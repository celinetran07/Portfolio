Split Decision

ToolsFigma, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python (Flask), MongoDB
Year2025

Thesis

How can a digital tool reduce the social friction of group decision-making, turning planning fatigue into something fair, fast, and even fun? Split Decision explores whether structured game-based interactions can replace the endless back-and-forth of group chats, giving everyone an equal voice without requiring any one person to carry the organizing burden.

Ideation

The concept emerged from the exhaustion of coordinating friend groups, a problem interviews with active social planners confirmed was widespread. People spent hours going back and forth in group chats without resolution, with the same person compromising every time.

I researched existing tools like Loomio, Kahoot, and Decido, and identified a gap for something more casual, mobile-first, and game-like. I explored game mechanics including elimination rounds, polls, wheel spinning, Rock Paper Scissors, coin flips, and tournament-style voting before narrowing to the features that felt most natural for friend groups. A user persona "Sacha Chacha", a social but planning-fatigued university student, anchored all design decisions.

Approach

I led UI/UX design, game logic implementation, and both rounds of usability testing. I conducted user interviews, built empathy maps, and created the lo-fi and hi-fi Figma prototypes for the main page, poll page, decision page, and join game page. I also handled deployment on Render and implemented the Rock-Paper-Scissors game logic, which went through two full redesigns after testing revealed a critical save-state confusion bug.

The project went through two formal testing cycles: one on the Figma prototype and one on the fully deployed website, with feedback directly driving each iteration.

Creative Works

The final product is a deployed web application where users can create a decision session, invite friends via a shared link, and resolve group choices through game-based voting. Features include a spinning wheel game with confetti winner reveal, an asynchronous Rock-Paper-Scissors system, a shared availability calendar, and a team-randomizer. The application is mobile-first, designed to be used in moments of social planning, on the go, in real time, with minimal friction.

In collaboration with Cathy Tham.
